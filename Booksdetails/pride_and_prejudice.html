Classic Literature, Romance, Social Commentary

Pride and Prejudice

Jane Austen

Book Description :

It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a single man in possession of a good fortune, must be in want of a wife.
— This famous opening line, narrated by Jane Austen’s omniscient narrator, introduces the central theme of Pride and Prejudice. As seen through the eyes of Elizabeth Bennet, the protagonist, the story explores her evolving relationship with the seemingly proud Mr. Darcy. Initially, Elizabeth judges Darcy to be arrogant and dismissive of her social class. However, over time, as both characters learn to overcome their initial biases and misunderstandings, their attraction grows.
Throughout the novel, other characters, such as Mr. Collins and Lady Catherine de Bourgh, add to the social commentary on marriage, status, and the expectations placed on women in society.
