The Great Gatsby

F. Scott Fitzgerald

Book Description :

Set in the summer of 1922, Nick Carraway narrates the story of his time in West Egg, Long Island.
The enigmatic Jay Gatsby, known for his lavish parties, is revealed to be deeply in love with Daisy Buchanan.
Gatsby's obsession with recreating his past with Daisy ultimately leads to tragedy.
The novel explores themes of wealth, the American Dream, and the hollowness of social status.
It is a critique of the 1920s, often called the "Roaring Twenties," and its pursuit of excess and pleasure.
